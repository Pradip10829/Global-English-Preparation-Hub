Overview

Why students choose TOEFL

  • Academic English focus for university admission goals.
  • Integrated tasks reward source comprehension and synthesis.
  • Section-based scoring helps target academic weaknesses directly.
Preparation tips
  • Spend time every week on lecture listening and academic note-taking.
  • Practice integrated writing with source comparison, not memorized essays.
  • Review reading by reasoning pattern, not only by topic.
  • Use clear speaking structures to stay organized under time limits.
Sections

The four TOEFL skill areas

Reading

Academic text analysis

Focus on inference, vocabulary in context, and summary-style questions.

Writing

Integrated and organized responses

Build concise synthesis instead of isolated idea lists.

Listening

Lecture note selection

Capture structure, purpose, and supporting examples without over-noting.

Speaking

Timed academic clarity

Use a predictable structure so responses stay coherent under pressure.

Scoring system
60-79

Emerging academic control with room to improve integration.

80-99

Competitive range for many academic programs.

100+

High target showing stronger synthesis and organization.

Common mistakes
  • Summarizing sources separately instead of synthesizing them.
  • Taking too many notes and missing lecture structure.
  • Speaking with weak organization despite having good ideas.
