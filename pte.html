Overview

Why students choose PTE

  • Fully computer-based workflow.
  • Integrated tasks can improve multiple scores at once.
  • Strong fit for repeatable practice and template-driven study.
Preparation tips
  • Prioritize repeat sentence, read aloud, and dictation tasks early.
  • Practice with a steady speaking pace instead of rushing.
  • Use templates only when they still sound relevant and flexible.
  • Review mistakes by question type, not only by skill.
Sections

The four PTE skill areas

Reading

Logic and speed

Practice re-order paragraphs, blanks, and sentence flow with sharper timing discipline.

Writing

Concise, repeatable structure

Focus on clean summary writing and short, controlled essay patterns.

Listening

Retention and dictation

Strengthen detail capture, note discipline, and sentence reconstruction.

Speaking

Fluency and microphone control

Build stable pronunciation, pacing, and confidence across speaking tasks.

Scoring system
50-58

Developing control with visible gaps in consistency.

65-72

Competitive score range for many pathways.

79+

High target with stronger fluency and task execution.

Common mistakes
  • Rushing through speaking tasks and hurting clarity.
  • Overusing rigid templates that do not fit the prompt.
  • Losing marks in dictation through spelling or missing function words.
